Farm & Homestead

A hobby farm.
Run by people who
are still figuring it out.

We brought nine
chickens from Ontario.
That was the beginning.

When we moved to Raven House, we had no farming background to speak of. What we had was a piece of land, a lot of curiosity, and four kids who were genuinely excited about animals.

The chickens came first — nine of them, all the way from Ontario. The goats arrived next. Then the ducks, the turkeys, the geese, the pigs. The horse was already here when we arrived. The cattle came later.

We've since lost count. It's a hobby farm, if we're being honest. Not a lot of real farming gets done here. What our freeloaders don't produce in profit, they more than make up for in sheer entertainment.

The residents

Lions and tigers and bears — well, not quite.

The Goats

Nigerian Dwarfs. Small, loud, and relentlessly charming. They produce milk, cause trouble, and follow the kids everywhere.

The Flock

Chickens, ducks, turkeys, and geese. Opinionated, productive on their own schedule, and the reason we're always finding feathers indoors.

The Horse & Cattle

The horse was here when we arrived and has been the property's steadiest presence. The cattle came later and are considerably harder to move.

The Calves

New arrivals every spring. The kids are always first to the barn. The calves are always worth losing sleep over.

Kidding Season

Every spring the goats kid and the whole farm gets louder. The babies are absurdly small and immediately convinced they run things.

The Cats & Others

Barn cats who have decided they are house cats. And surely something else quietly freeloading that we haven't fully accounted for yet.

Life on the farm

What each season brings.

Spring

Kidding season. New calves. The first eggs after a slow winter. Mud everywhere. The pigs arrive. Everything happens at once and nobody sleeps much.

Summer

Long days. Kids outside from morning until dark. The goats on the move. River walks. Peak egg production from the girls — when they feel like it.

Autumn

Harvest. The pigs leave. Preserving. The valley turns and the mornings get cold fast. Firewood. Preparing everything for what's coming.

Winter

Slower. The animals need more tending in the cold. The ravens are more visible against the snow. Good time to plan, make things, and remember why we moved here.
